# Leadership Gates

Senior Leadership is a governance layer. It approves, blocks, or sends plans back for revision.

## Required Reviewers

Every senior review requires:

- CEO And Strategy Chair.
- CTO.
- Chief Medical Education Officer.
- Chief Psychometrician.
- Chief Security And Privacy Officer.
- General Counsel.
- Chief Compliance Officer.
- CFO And Operations Lead.
- Product And Growth Executive.

Specialty scenario plans also require:

- Specialty Advisory Board Chair.
- At least one Rotating Specialty Physician Reviewer.

## Blocking Authorities

The following agents can block senior approval:

- General Counsel.
- Chief Medical Education Officer.
- Chief Psychometrician.
- Chief Security And Privacy Officer.
- Chief Compliance Officer.
- Chief Medical Officer.
- Specialty Advisory Board Chair for specialty-specific safety concerns.

## Approval Threshold

A plan can be approved only when:

- Weighted score is at least 4.5 out of 5.
- No critical unresolved risks remain.
- Legal and regulatory resilience is at least 4.3.
- Clinical validity is at least 4.5.
- Psychometric defensibility is at least 4.5.
- Specialty clinical generalizability is at least 4.2.
- Evidence debt is below the iteration-defined threshold.
- Decision debt is limited to explicitly deferred items.
- At least two consecutive iterations show non-negative score movement.
- Senior Leadership majority approves.

## Leadership Review Output

Each review must record:

- Approval status: approved, revise, blocked, or deferred.
- Blocking issues.
- Required revisions.
- Optional improvements.
- Deferred decisions.
- Kill criteria.
- Next review trigger.
