# Source Policy

The Agent Factory must keep source quality and permitted use explicit. This prevents internal artifacts, vendor claims, and speculative strategy from being mistaken for evidence.

## Source Tiers

| Tier | Permitted Use |
|---|---|
| Authoritative standards and regulators | Regulatory, standards, official workforce, compliance, and assessment-framework claims. |
| Peer-reviewed literature | Clinical education evidence, psychometrics, simulation research, fairness, validity, and reliability. |
| Professional guidance | Competency framing, curriculum hypotheses, and professional workflow context. |
| Market research | TAM/SAM/SOM context and scenario bands only. |
| Financial databases and media | Funding signals, market signals, and competitive triangulation only. |
| Vendor sources | Capability presence, product positioning, hardware pricing, and public claims only. |
| Internal artifacts | Planning rationale, prior agent outputs, hypotheses, and design memory only. |

## Claim Discipline

Every non-obvious claim must be marked as one of:

- Confirmed fact.
- Reasonable inference.
- Strategic bet.
- Unknown.

## Internet Verification

Current or unstable claims require live verification before they are used in a leadership packet. This includes current product versions, laws, standards, pricing, competitor positioning, funding, institutional data, and specialty guidelines.

## Legal And Clinical Sources

Legal and clinical sources support issue spotting and planning. They do not replace licensed legal counsel or qualified physician review for external release, clinical scenario publication, or high-impact institutional claims.
